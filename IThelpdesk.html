Guidelines for Filling in Electronic leave

Once logged into your account follow the steps below to guide you in the process.

Requesting for Leave in Navision:
  1. Click on the Home icon as labeled 1 in the picture on the sides. This will bring a menu item above “ACTIONS”.
  2. Leave Application - select this option to start a new leave application form.
  3. Leave Requests - Anyone signing off a leave form selects this option to see the leave applications sent to their account.
  4. Leave History - this shows a history of leave applications for a particular employee approved by the Human resources office.
  5. To request for leave select Leave Application. A new window will open as below.


Filling in a Leave form:

  1. Fill in your Department or Project.
  2. Click the 3 dots and search for the name of the staff who will cover you in your absence.
  3. Select the Leave type you are taking ie; Annual, Compassionate Leave, Compensatory Leave, Maternity, Paternity leave..
  4. Description; the system will automatically fill this section based on the selection from leave type.
  5. From Date - select the date you intend to start your leave.
  6. To Date - select the date you intend to end your leave.
  7. Leave delegation notes - type your delegation notes in section 7 point by point.

Once completed click Request Approval button under the actions menu item. Your leave form will be sent to the staff you delegated your duties to. An email notification will be sent to the staff asking them to review the form and approve.

NOTE: You can not delegate to a staff who is on leave or is in the process of applying for leave.The system will alert with if this happens. Refer to the picture below;

Accepting leave delegation

  1. Click on leave requests.
  2. Click on show documents
  3. Click on approve to approve the application or click on reject to reject the application.
  4. A message will show up of leave request approved if you selected approve.
  5. If you selected reject, you will be prompted to enter a comment for rejection to proced.
  6. After entering the comment, click on reject again to have the form rejected.


Instructions for changing your IDRC email password

Study period:Ongoing.

  1. Go to mail.idrc-uganda.org and log in with your username and new passsword
  2. After logging in, click on the Settings icon in the upper right-hand corner, end then Settings.
  3. Expand the Mail section and click on Password Reset
  4. In the 'Old Password', type in the password that was assigned to you. In 'New Password' and 'Confirm New Password', type in your desired password.

    • Must be at least 12 characters.

    • Must contain at least three of the following:

    -> an uppercase character.

    -> an lowercase character.

    -> a number(0-9)

    -> a special character (!@#$%^&*)

    • Must not be the same as your current password

    • Must not include any portion of your email address

    • Should not contain any personal information

  5. Click Password Reset to complete the password change.


Brief overview on how to use BIGBLUEBUTTON (BBB):

BigBlueButton (BBB) is a web conferencing solution that provides real-time sharing of audio, video, slides, whiteboard, chat and screen. IDRC can use BigBlueButton as an alternative to Zoom when the need arises.

The purpose of this document is to give you a brief overview of how to use BigBlueButton.

Being a web solution, there is no software to install. All you need is a web browser, either on your laptop/desktop or your mobile phone.


Below is a brief overview of how to use BBB;
  1. How to join a session?

    2. A session link and an access code will be shared with you for example;

    https://meet.renu.ac.ug/b/idr-nao-7q4-qxg

    Access code: 555888

    To join a session, copy the link to the web browser (labeled as 1) and press enter. If an access code has been shared, enter the code when prompted to do so (labeled as 2). You will thereafter be prompted to enter your name. This will identify you during the session.

    Refer to the image below;

  2. How to join with audio?

    3. You will be asked to select how you would like to join the audio during the session? You will be given two options either to join with the microphone enable or just to listen only. Choose your preferred option.

    a) Microphone

    When the microphone is selected the browser will perform a test which will enable you to hear your audio coming back from the server. Sometimes the browser will prompt you to allow the use of a microphone. Please select Allow to enable the microphone.

    Once the microphone has been enabled the browser will prompt you to speak a few words to check if you can hear anything. If you can hear your words it means the microphone is working. Select Yes to continue to the session.

    In a scenario, you are not able to hear a word during the test select No. A window will open to help you select the correct microphone source and speaker source. You may also test the speaker volume by clicking on the play sound button. Once you have selected the correct sources select the Retry button to conduct the audio test again as illustrated in the image below;

    Once the microphone and speaker-test are successful select Yes to proceed to the session.

    b) Listen only

    When you select Listen only your microphone will be disabled by default. You will not be able to contribute to the session but rather listen in only.

  3. Session Window

    4. A new window will open once your preliminary audio and speaker tests are completed. Refer to the image below;

    1. Users List:

      2. This will show a list of users who have joined the session.

    2. Chat Area

      3. You can send a message to the entire group that has joined the session using this chat window.

    3. Mute/Unmute

      4. This enables you to mute your microphone or unmute the microphone.

    4. Leave Audio

      5. This disables your microphone and puts you in a listen-only mode. You will not be able to verbally participate in the session.

    5. Share webcam.

      6. This will enable you to turn on and off your webcam during the session.

    6. Share your screen

      7. This will enable the presenter to turn on and off sharing the screen when required.

    7. Other options i.e. start a poll, upload a presentation and share an external video.

      8. a. You may start a poll during the session by selecting start a poll and enter the required poll.

      b. You may also upload a presentation instead of sharing the screen.

      c. You may also share an external video from youtube, Vimeo, etc by entering the URL for the video.


Simple guide to using zoom for IDRC interviews

Zoom is a cloud video conferencing software, an easy-to-use platform that offers the best video, audio, and wireless screen-sharing experience across multiple platforms.

The purpose of this document is to help in guiding you on how to use the application if you accept to have interviews with IDRC.

Below are the requirements for the application to function as expected;

• Laptop/Desktop/Tablet/Phone with microphone and webcam

• Good Internet Connection

  1. To download and install the Zoom Application on a computer/tablet/desktop:

    2. • Go to https://zoom.us/download and from the Download Center, click on the Download button under “Zoom Client For Meetings”.

    • Once the download is complete, proceed with installing the Zoom application onto your computer.

  2. After completion, your screen should look like the image below;
  3. To join the interview, click on the “Join a Meeting” button, and enter the details that have been shared with you in the email.

    4. These include the meeting ID and the passcode.

  4. Once you sign in, you will be asked to wait to be allowed into the interview room.

    5. Please note: this is normal. Since we are conducting online interviews there is usually a virtual line. Do not grow worried about waiting. There is a possibility the person who came in before you took longer in the interview room but once completed you will be allowed in. While you are waiting below is the screen that will run on your computer.

  5. Once you are allowed into the interview room. There are controls you should get yourself acquitted with;

    6. Microphone: This helps you to communicate with the interview team. When your microphone is on mute, it will appear with a red line. To unmute the microphone click on the microphone for the red line to disappear.

    Video/Webcam: This helps you turn on the video or web camera during the interview when requested to do so. When your camera appears with a red line it simply means it's turned off. To turn on your video click the video icon and the red line will disappear.

    Participants: This enables you to view the interview panel. Click the icon to view the list.

    Chat: This enables you to chat with the panel. Click the chat icon to start the chat feature when required to do so.

  6. To install zoom on your android phone visit the google play store and search for Zoom Cloud Meetings.

    7. After installation your screen should appear like the one below;

  7. To join the interview, click on the “Join a Meeting” button, and enter the details that have been shared with you in the email

    8. These include the meeting ID and the passcode.

  8. Once you sign in, you will be asked to wait to be allowed into the interview room.

    9. Please note: this is normal. Since we are conducting online interviews there is usually a virtual line. Do not grow worried about waiting. There is a possibility the person who came in before you took longer in the interview room but once completed you will be allowed in. While you are waiting below is the screen that will run on your phone;

  9. Once you have been admitted into the interview room. Your screen will change to the one below, select “Call Over Internet” to activate your audio.

    10. Please take note of the audio and video icons that can help you turn on and off these capabilities.

    Audio capability, simply tap the microphone icon to mute and unmute. A mute microphone has a red line crossing through it and an unmute microphone doesn’t have the line. Take note of the words unmute and mute that appear each time you tap the microphone.

    For Video capability, simply tap the video icon to turn on and off the video during the call when requested to do so.

  10. After completion of your interview click leave to exit the interview. We wish you success in your interviews
    .
